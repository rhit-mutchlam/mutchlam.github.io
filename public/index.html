About Erin

A graduate of the University of Miami’s Frost School of Music, Erin is an up-and-coming jazz artist and audio engineer. Over her many years studying music and engineering, Erin has accumulated many professional skills such as trumpet performance, composition, arranging, teaching, recording, mixing, mastering, audio editing, and audio plugin design. At the Frost School of Music Erin studied with Grammy Award winning trumpeter Brian Lynch and performed with the university’s top ensemble, the renowned Concert Jazz Band. Erin was also fortunate to work with many other incredible trumpeters during her time at the Frost School such as Jean Caze, John Daversa, Ingrid Jenson, Brad Goode, Michael Rodriguez, and Marquis Hill. Erin has shared the stage with many great artists such as David Liebman, Frank Greene, Dave Holland, Nick Smart, and Steve Miller, among many others.

Some of Erin’s notable accolades include the 2018 Laurie Frink Career Grant and the 2019 Student Downbeat Award for Best Engineered Studio Recording. She was also a semi-finalist in the National Trumpet Competition Jazz Division for three consecutive years starting in 2017. Her professional audio engineering releases include the multi-award winning album Arethusa Falls (2019), by saxophonist Adam Claussen, and the soundtrack to the award winning movie July Rising (2019), scored by Seth Crail and directed by Chauncey Crail.

Trumpet

Erin’s musical style is best described as ECM-esque. Having long admired the European record label’s aesthetic, it is now the main influence and focus of Erin’s trumpet playing. In addition to the inspiration of ECM artists such as Kenny Wheeler, Ralph Towner, and Tomasz Stanko, Erin also draws influence from many styles of Brazilian music - specifically a traditional style of Brazilian music called choro. In 2018 Erin was named one of the two recipients of the Laurie Frink Career Grant. This grant has enabled her to travel to Brazil for two months to learn choro from the great choro artists of Rio de Janeiro such as Silverio Pontés and Aquiles de Moraes.

Audio Engineering

Erin began delving into audio engineering during her first year at the University of Miami when she started recording demos for her friend’s ensembles in the practice rooms of the Frost School of Music. Six years later, she has now recorded, mixed, and mastered an award winning jazz album as well as an award winning film. Her audio engineering is not limited to audio production and post-production. Erin also has experience with audio editing, live sound recording and reinforcement, and even audio plugin design. For more information on Erin’s audio engineering endeavors, please visit her Audio Engineering page.
